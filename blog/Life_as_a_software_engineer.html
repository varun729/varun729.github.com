Life as a software engineer

A new subject has always attracted my attention, and I have always spent sometime in exploring and learning atleast something about it. During high school I was very enthusiastic about mathematics and used to spend hours solving problems and reading about it. I was sure of how I would spend the rest of my life (solving problems). Well, had I not got interested into the idea of becoming an engineer, I might well be doing it. Anyways, I studied Electrical Engineering and studied electromagnetics upto a considerable depth. I have since then delved more in to computers, and now I am a software engineer. It is a very concise life history, but important to me.

I have been working as a software engineer since August 2010, and luckily in a company run by professors and not managers. So I still spend a lot of time studying new things. Although there is still a lot of pressure of delivering products, but I always am able to find a problem other than work, which is more interesting than the work.

Most of the time I have think of ways in which a particular feature can be embedded into the software, and it often requires using some available library. But recently I have had the opportunity to work on more interesting stuff. Creating a suffix array for the human genome sounds much more fun than anything. I learnt a lot from it. There is a similar challenging task where I need to create a graph (plot) for some metric for the human genome. Now these are times when you actually feel like an engineer.

Working at Strand (Strand Life Sciences), has many advantages. You get to attend lectures by one of the best professors, Ramesh Hariharan. Recently he discussed about how to find the Least Common Ancestor in a tree. This is a very interesting problem and the way he simplified it, really boggles me. His lectures are always enlightening and interesting. And you get motivated to do something. I am currently implementing the algorithm, and will soon update it here on the website.
